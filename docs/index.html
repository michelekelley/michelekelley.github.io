Research

Hyperpolarized Xenon

The focus of my research is hyperpolarized nuclei for nuclear magnetic resonance (NMR) and magnetic resonance imaging (MRI). Specifically, I work with the noble gas xenon, which is "hyperpolarized" by transferring spin polarization from a high-power laser. This process is called spin-exchange optical pumping (SEOP). The result is the same inert noble gas, but it behaves like it is in a magnetic field much larger than we can produce in the lab. NMR/MRI with hyperpolarized xenon is interesting because we can do things like image the lungs to study ventilation defects, quantify brown adipose tissue (aka brown fat - a metabolically active tissue), and perform measurements at lower magnetic field strengths, among other emerging applications.
