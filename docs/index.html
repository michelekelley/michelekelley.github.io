I am a PhD candidate at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill where I hold an interdisplinary Royster Fellowship and a NSF Graduate Research Fellowship. I will be graduating in May 2023.
My graduate research has involved theoretical, computational, and experimental work on nuclear and electonic spin systems and developing instruments with optics and electronics. A link to my publications and my github is in my contact info above.
I enjoy taking on new projects, have strong problem-solving skills, and enjoy working on a team.
I am committed to diversity, equity, and inclusion in STEM. I am a mentor and program coordinator for my department's graduate-to-undergraduate mentoring program for underrepresented racial minorities.
